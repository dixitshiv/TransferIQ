STABILITY DATA SUMMARY — Valsartan Hard Gelatin Capsules 80mg

Protocol Reference: FAR-STAB-VAL-001, Rev 2
ICH Q1A(R2) Compliant Study Design

STORAGE CONDITIONS STUDIED (FAREVA BATCHES ONLY):

1. LONG-TERM STABILITY — 25°C / 60% RH
   Container: HDPE bottle + 2g silica gel desiccant (Fareva packaging)
   Duration: 24 months complete
   Timepoints: Initial, 3M, 6M, 12M, 18M, 24M

   Results Summary:
   Timepoint | Assay (%) | Dissolution (%) | RS Total (%) | WC (%)  | NDMA (ng/dose) | DSC Tg (°C)
   Initial   |  100.2    |   82.4          |   0.10       |  0.72   |   28            |   74.2
   3 months  |  100.0    |   81.8          |   0.11       |  0.74   |   29            |   73.8
   6 months  |   99.7    |   81.3          |   0.13       |  0.76   |   30            |   73.5
   12 months |   99.5    |   79.6          |   0.17       |  0.81   |   32            |   73.1
   18 months |   99.3    |   77.4          |   0.22       |  0.84   |   35            |   72.8
   24 months |   99.0    |   75.2          |   0.28       |  0.88   |   38            |   72.2
   Status: PASSING all specifications at 24 months (dissolution approaching Q=70% at 24M — margin: 5.2%)

   CRITICAL NOTE: Dissolution at 24 months (75.2%) has a margin of only 5.2% above the Q=70% limit.
   Amorphous valsartan Tg is declining (72.2°C at 24M vs. 74.2°C initial) — indicating slow,
   progressive recrystallisation. Thermo Fisher site environmental controls are critical.

2. ACCELERATED STABILITY — 40°C / 75% RH
   Container: HDPE bottle + 2g silica gel desiccant
   Duration: 6 months complete

   Results Summary:
   Timepoint | Assay (%) | Dissolution (%) | RS Total (%) | NDMA (ng/dose) | DSC Tg (°C)
   Initial   |  100.2    |   82.4          |   0.10       |   28            |   74.2
   3 months  |   99.5    |   74.8          |   0.22       |   41            |   70.1
   6 months  |   98.7    |   68.1          |   0.38       |   47            |   65.3
   Status: FAIL at 6 months — dissolution 68.1% is BELOW Q=70% specification
   DSC Tg at 65.3°C confirms significant amorphous → crystalline conversion at accelerated conditions.
   NDMA increased from 28 to 47 ng/dose (still within 96 ng/day limit but upward trend noted).

3. INTERMEDIATE STABILITY — 30°C / 65% RH
   Duration: 12 months complete
   Dissolution: 78.4% at 12M (within spec; no Tg shift detected by DSC)
   Status: PASSING

4. PHOTOSTABILITY — ICH Q1B
   NOT CONDUCTED on capsules in final packaging configuration.
   NOTE: CRITICAL GAP — Valsartan is not known to be photosensitive, but photostability study
   on capsules in HDPE bottle has not been performed. Required for regulatory submission.

CRITICAL GAPS IN STABILITY PACKAGE:
- Accelerated stability FAILURE at 40°C/75% RH — dissolution below specification at 6 months
  This demonstrates high sensitivity of amorphous valsartan to temperature and humidity.
  Thermo Fisher site humidity and temperature controls must be demonstrated equivalent to Fareva.
- NDMA upward trend at accelerated conditions — capsule shell supplier change (ACG vs. Capsugel)
  may alter nitrosamine profile; accelerated stability with new supplier must be generated.
- No stability data from Thermo Fisher batches — 0 of 3 confirmatory batches initiated
- DSC capability gap at Thermo Fisher — Tg monitoring not possible without equipment or external CRO
- Photostability study not conducted in final container

SHELF LIFE CLAIM: 30 months (based on Fareva data; dissolution margin is narrow)
NOTE: The accelerated stability failure is a significant regulatory risk for site change submissions.
FDA and EMA will scrutinise amorphous form stability at the receiving site.
A comparative dissolution study between Fareva and Thermo Fisher batches is strongly recommended
before NDA/ANDA supplement submission.
