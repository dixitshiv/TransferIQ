ANALYTICAL METHODS PACKAGE — Ibuprofen Film-Coated Tablets 400mg

1. ASSAY — HPLC Method
   Reference: CAT-AM-IBU-001, Rev 2
   Instrument: Waters Alliance e2695 HPLC (Catalent)
   Column: C18, 250 x 4.6mm, 5μm (Zorbax SB-C18)
   Mobile phase: 45:55 phosphate buffer pH 7.4 : acetonitrile
   Flow rate: 1.5 mL/min
   Detection: UV at 221nm
   System suitability: RSD NMT 1.0%, tailing factor NMT 2.0
   Validation: Fully validated per ICH Q2(R1) — specificity, linearity (0.05–0.15 mg/mL), accuracy, precision
   Method transfer status: PARTIAL — data exchange complete; equivalence on Lonza Waters ACQUITY UPLC system
   not yet confirmed (UPLC vs. HPLC column chemistry equivalence needed)

2. DISSOLUTION — USP Apparatus II (Surfactant Medium)
   Reference: CAT-AM-IBU-002, Rev 1
   Apparatus: USP Type II (paddle)
   Medium: 900 mL 0.1N HCl + 0.5% sodium lauryl sulfate (required for BCS Class II sink conditions)
   Speed: 75 rpm
   Temperature: 37.0 ± 0.5°C
   Timepoint: 30 min
   Acceptance: Q = 75%
   Detection: UV at 221nm
   Method transfer status: NOT COMPLETED
   NOTE: BCS Class II dissolution with surfactant is highly sensitive to paddle speed (±5 rpm effect)
   and vessel geometry. Full inter-laboratory transfer qualification required at Lonza.
   Lonza apparatus qualification records not received.

3. PARTICLE SIZE DISTRIBUTION — Laser Diffraction
   Reference: CAT-AM-IBU-003, Rev 1
   Instrument: Malvern Mastersizer 2000 (Catalent) — wet dispersion
   Dispersant: Mineral oil + 0.1% lecithin
   Specification: d90 < 150 μm, d50 30–80 μm, d10 > 5 μm
   Validation: Validated (repeatability, intermediate precision)
   Method transfer status: NOT STARTED
   NOTE: Lonza site uses Sympatec HELOS (dry dispersion); instrument equivalence study
   required as wet vs. dry PSD methods may give systematically different d90 results.
   This is a critical CQA for Ibuprofen dissolution performance.

4. RELATED SUBSTANCES — HPLC Gradient
   Reference: CAT-AM-IBU-004, Rev 1
   Column: Same as assay; gradient: 10–90% acetonitrile over 30 min
   Known impurities: Ibuprofen related compound A (USP), 4-isobutylacetophenone
   Limits: Individual unknown NMT 0.05%; total impurities NMT 0.20%
   Validation: Complete
   Method transfer status: COMPLETE

5. UNIFORMITY OF DOSAGE UNITS — Content Uniformity
   Method: USP <905> (compendial)
   No transfer validation required per site policy.
   Status: Complete

6. HARDNESS / FRIABILITY
   Methods: USP <1217> and USP <1216> (compendial)
   No transfer required.
